Project Management

Learn the key principles, tools, and techniques to plan, execute, and deliver successful projects.

Course Overview

This course offers a practical introduction to the discipline of project management. You will explore the project lifecycle from initiation to closing, develop skills in scheduling, budgeting, and risk management, and learn how to use project management software. Through real-world examples and hands-on exercises, you will gain confidence in managing projects of various sizes and complexities. Whether you are starting your career or seeking to formalize your experience, this course will help you lead projects effectively.

Course Schedule

Date Topic Description
Week 1 Introduction to Project Management Key concepts, terminology, and the project lifecycle.
Week 2 Planning and Scheduling Developing project plans, timelines, and work breakdown structures.
Week 3 Budgeting and Resource Management Estimating costs and allocating resources effectively.
Week 4 Risk and Quality Management Identifying risks, ensuring quality, and maintaining control.
Week 5 Project Execution and Closing Managing execution, monitoring progress, and closing projects successfully.

Prerequisites

No prior project management experience is needed. A general understanding of business operations is helpful.
